TREES

TRAVIS DAWSON GARCIA

I
THINK
THAT    I
SHALL NEVER
SEE,   A      POEM
 LOVELY  AS A  TREE
A  TREE  WHOSE  HUNGRY
MOUNT IS PRIEST, AGAINST
THE EASTH'S SWEET FLOWING
BREAST A TREE THAT LOOKS AT
GOOD ALL DAY, AND LISFTS HER LEAFY
ARMRS      TO   PRAY A TREE THAT MAY IN
SUMMER      WEAR   A    NEST OF ROBINS IN
HAIT      UPON      WHOSE    BOSOM         SNOW
HAS LAIN WHOSE INTIMATELY LIVES WITH RAIN
PORMS ARE MADE
BY FOOLS LIKE ME
BUT ONLY GOD CAN
MAKE A TREE
